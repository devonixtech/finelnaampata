Overview
This document defines every question asked during the NAAMPATA business sign-up flow, in the exact order they appear. Each step specifies the field type, whether it is required or optional, all available options, and implementation notes for the developer.

Design Principles
Questions are organised into logical steps — one topic per step. Do not mix unrelated questions on one screen.
Required fields must be completed before the business can advance to the next step.
Optional fields can be skipped and completed later from the business profile settings.
Steps with many options must use scrollable checkbox lists — never dropdowns for multi-select.
Progress indicator must be visible throughout the entire sign-up flow showing current step / total steps.
Every step must have a "Save & Continue" button and a "Back" button.
The entire flow must be completable on mobile with no horizontal scrolling.
All structured answers are stored in the database as structured data — not free text — for AI features and analytics.

Step 7 — Business Address
Step 7  Where is your business located?
Required
Enter the full address of your business. This is publicly displayed on your profile and used for radius-based search. Use the autocomplete to find your address quickly.
Field type:  Address form with Google Places Autocomplete

Fields
Field
Details
Country
Required. Dropdown — full countries list (all countries worldwide).
Street Address
Required. Text input with Google Places Autocomplete (triggers after 3 characters, 300ms debounce, session tokens mandatory).
City / Town
Required. Auto-filled from Places Autocomplete. Editable.
State / Province / Region
Optional. Field label and presence dynamically set by country (libaddressinput dataset). See Address Localisation Spec.
Zip / Postal Code
Optional. Field label dynamically set by country. Not shown for countries without postal codes (e.g. Pakistan, UAE). Format validated server-side.
Landmark / Additional Info
Optional. Free text. Max 100 characters. Helper: "Floor number, building name, nearest landmark, etc."

Address fields are dynamically rendered based on country selection using the Google libaddressinput dataset (github.com/google/libaddressinput). Never hardcode field labels. Country selection drives the entire address form structure. See Address Form Spec for full implementation details.

Step 8 — Confirm Your Location on Map
Step 8  Confirm your location on the map
Required
After the address is entered, show a map with a pin at the detected location. The business can drag the pin to the exact position if needed. GPS auto-detect button also available.
Field type:  Interactive map with draggable pin

Behaviour
Auto-place pin at the coordinates returned by the address geocoding queue (BullMQ).
Business can drag the pin to correct position if the auto-placement is inaccurate.
"Detect My Location" button — uses device GPS (when-in-use permission only, never at launch).
If GPS is denied: fallback to manual city selection, then manual pin drag.
Coordinates are stored permanently after registration. Geocoding API is never called again for the same business.
Duplicate location check runs here — if another business is within 10m with a similar name or phone, show a confirmation prompt (not a hard block).

After Sign-Up — Listing Goes Live
Listings are approved and go live automatically after sign-up is completed. There is no manual approval step. Remove the approval queue entirely.

Post Sign-Up Profile Completion
After the listing goes live, the business is taken to their dashboard with a Profile Completion progress bar showing how complete their profile is. Incomplete optional sections show a prompt to complete them for a stronger profile score.

Profile score is calculated as a percentage and displayed on the business dashboard.
Higher profile completion = better search ranking (all else being equal).
Each incomplete optional section shows a "Complete now" button.
Businesses can edit any field from their profile settings at any time after registration.

Duplicate Business Detection
Run duplicate detection during Step 8 (Map Pin) and again on final submission. Never hard-block — only prompt for confirmation.

Data Storage Requirements
All structured question answers (Steps 2–6, 14, 15) must be stored as structured enum arrays in the database — never as free text strings.
This is required for AI categorisation, search filtering, analytics, and future feature development.
Each field must have a defined schema before development begins.
